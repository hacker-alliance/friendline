Neighbor line

917-341-7837 everyday, 3pm EST

Have a friendly chat in strange times.

How Does It Work?

Simply. Designed for everyone

Everyday at 3:00 pm (EST), Neighborline is open for calls.
Dial the number to be placed on the line with another caller for a chat.
Think of Neighborline as a way of striking up small spontaneous conversations.

Community Guidelines

A little kindness goes a long way

  • There is NO PLACE for hatred or intolerance on Neighborline. If you encounter this, HANG UP & REPORT IT to us.
  • DO NOT REVEAL PERSONAL DETAILS, including your telephone number. Neighborline is designed to keep your number safe.
  • This is NOT A CRISIS LINE. If you need help or critical support, look at the resources provided below.
  • BE KIND. Build bridges. Our circumstances may be different but everyone is struggling right now.

Feeling Shy?

That's okay, it happens to the best of us

Here are some tips for making good conversation.

  • Keep it brief! A small conversation can be a beautiful thing.
  • Bring your gratitude. Think of one thing that you are grateful for, and share it.
  • Ask a good question. What have you read recently? Have you seen a fun movie? Tell your neighbor.
  • Be vulnerable. It's ok to have a bad day. Expressing- within reason- it can help.
  • Don't try to solve anyone's problems. It's ok to just listen. In fact, it's often better than advice!

Who's on the Line?

Your neighbors & fellow New Yorkers

Neighborline creates an opportunity for daily connection in a challenging time.

This daily call can be especially helpful for localized communities in isolation who may not have access to highly-networked circles online, like the elderly or those without access to the internet. Truly, Neighborline is for anyone who has a phone.

Problems? Questions?

Uh-oh. Let's try to fix it

IF YOU HAVE A PROBLEM OR QUESTION You can email us or call us at 917-341-7837 and press 2 when prompted to leave a voicemail. If you have a media request, say hello.
PLEASE REMEMBER Neighborline is for our community and staffed by volunteers. We will make our best effort to get back to you promptly.

Why Did We Make This?

Something to look forward to, daily

Normally, New Yorkers share impromtu exchanges as we move through our city. As we stay home, we need to find new ways of being neighborly. Like a cheer at 7pm, it's important for us to create rituals and have something to look forward to everyday.

At the moment, Neighborline has is serving New York. We hope Neighborline can be a model for other areas around the country and the world. We’re providing all the documentation and resources for Neighborline to inspire others to replicate, reimagine, and rediscover the magic of a good, old-fashioned phone call.
